About the University of Caloocan City

Established in 1971, the University of Caloocan City (UCC) is a premier local public university. Formerly known as Caloocan City Community College, it has grown from 42 students to over 13,000, standing as a beacon of "100% Free Tuition" and academic excellence for the "Batang Kankaloo."

University History

UCC’s journey began on July 1, 1971. Originally a Community College with a budget of just ₱23,400, it evolved into the Caloocan City Polytechnic College in 1975. By February 9, 2004, it officially attained University status.

Under the leadership of Mayor Oscar "Oca" Malapitan and currently Mayor Dale Gonzalo "Along" Malapitan, the university has undergone a "Mega Infrastructure" transformation, ensuring that "Basta sa Caloocan, walang batang maiiwan."
